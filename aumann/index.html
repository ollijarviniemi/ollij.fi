How to play

You and a teammate sit at the table. From a standard 52-card deck, each of you gets 5 cards — but you can't see each other's:

Three random "condition cards" are drawn and shown to both of you:

Your shared question: looking at all 10 cards combined, is at least one of the 3 conditions satisfied? Each of you forms a belief about the probability.

You both then simultaneously place a token on the board, in the column that matches your belief. The board's middle row tells you what each column pays out:

Once you've both placed, you see each other's token. Maybe your teammate is more confident than you, and they have probably good reason — that might shift your belief. You then place a second token (round 2), wherever you now think the right column is:

The cards are revealed, the conditions checked. Each token scores by its column: if any condition is met you get the top number; otherwise the bottom one. The middle column gives 7 either way — the safe pick when you're unsure.

Tokens score independently. The team total is your two scores plus your teammate's two — 40 points possible per game. After many games, the average matters more than any single round.

The sidebar tracks your scores and, on hover, what two perfect Bayesians would have scored on the same hands. After the reveal you'll also see two dashed lines on the board showing the ideal Bayesian's round-1 and round-2 column for each of you, with a curved arrow tracing how a perfect updater would have moved.
